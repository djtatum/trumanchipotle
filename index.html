I
October 12, 1989

The foil was wrapped in a way that suggested another dimension. When I peeled it back, I did not find beans or rice. I found a small key, cold to the touch, and the scent of Douglas firs. The grill master looked at me, but his eyes were like television static.

November 3, 1989

He is sitting in the corner booth. He looks exactly like me, but he is wearing a dark suit. He ordered a burrito bowl, double steak. He has been eating it for three days. The bowl is never empty. Sometimes he whispers: "The owls are not what they seem, but the guacamole is extra."

December 15, 1989

The red curtains in the back of the kitchen are not on the blueprint. The manager claims they have always been there, but the salsa bar is whispering in reverse. I asked for the medium salsa, and the clerk replied in a voice that sounded like a record played backwards: "Yrt eht reppep."

January 20, 1990

A blue rose. It grew from a discarded napkin. If you listen closely, it plays a song from a radio station that went off the air in 1957. The sound is beautiful, but it makes me forget my own name. We are like the dreamer who dreams, and then lives inside the dream. Who is the dreamer?
